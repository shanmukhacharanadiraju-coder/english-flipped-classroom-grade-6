Background & Storyline

  • Mithila (Madhubani) paintings ‚Äî an ancient folk tradition practiced mostly by village women.
  • Paintings were used during weddings, festivals, and religious rituals.
  • A drought and poverty made survival difficult; officials suggested selling art on paper.
  • Women began selling paintings; their art reached national and international markets.
  • Art became a source of income, pride, and recognition for women painters.

Main Themes & Messages

  1. Empowerment: Art as a tool for economic independence.
  2. Tradition & Change: Respecting tradition while adapting to new markets.
  3. Community: Collective creativity and shared cultural identity.
  4. Gender & Recognition: Women gaining status through their skill.
